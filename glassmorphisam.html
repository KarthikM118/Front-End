Black holes are formed from the remnants of massive stars that have ended their life cycles. When a star with a mass greater than approximately 20 times that of the Sun exhausts its nuclear fuel, it may undergo a supernova explosion, collapsing under its own gravity into a singularity—a point of infinite density—and creating a black hole.
